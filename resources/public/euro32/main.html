Transport to Aalto University

Aalto University is located in Otaniemi, Espoo about 6 km to the north-west of Helsinki city center. The new, modern metro connection opened in 2017 provides a quick and easy way to get to Aalto from central locations in Helsinki using the public transportation pass included in the registration fee.

From Helsinki city centre the Tapiola and Matinkylä metro lines will bring delegates to Aalto University. The station is called Aalto University. The Metro runs every 2.5 minutes at Aalto University station during the rush hour, and every 4–5 minutes at other times. The journey from Kamppi to Aalto University takes 11 minutes.

At the Aalto University Metro station, there are two exits: Otaniementie (Exit A) and Tietotie (Exit B). Use Otaniementie exit to access the conference venue. When returning from Aalto University to Helsinki city centre please take the Vuosaari or the Mellunmäki metro lines.

Included in your registration is a four-day pass (Sunday-Wednesday) for public transportation including buses, trams, metro, commuter trains, and Suomenlinna ferry on zones A, B, and C. These zones cover for example cities of Helsinki,Espoo, and Vantaa in which the airport is located.

  1. To activate your pass please download the HSL app from your app store.

  1. Select buy a ticket

  2. Select ticket with a code

  3. Enter the ticket code provided to you in your final confirmation email and select continue.

Please note the pass is valid for maximum 4 days only. If you activate the pass in advance of Sunday 3 July 2022 you will need to purchase additional day tickets to use the metro once the 4 day pass expires.

Coffee breaks

Coffee breaks will be served in Väre, Level 1 and in three locations in Otakaari 1: Levels 1 and 2 in the main lobby and in the exhibition area.

Lunches

A buffet lunch will be served in 3 restaurants on campus between 11:30 – 14:00 from Monday to Wednesday.

  1. Alvari Restaurant – Level 1, Otakaari 1 Building – Blue Lunch Tickets

  2. A Bloc Restaurant – Level 2, Väre Building – Yellow Lunch Tickets

  3. Reima Restaurant – Level 2, Dipoli Building – Pink Lunch Tickets

In order to manage queues and capacities in the restaurants delegates will be pre-allocated to one of the three restaurants for the duration of the conference. Delegates should only attend the restaurant they have been allocated to for lunch. Lunch vouchers will be provided at the time of registration. The colour of your lunch ticket indicates the restaurant you have been allocated. Delegates will be required to hand in a voucher each day to receive their lunch. Lunch will not be served to delegates without a voucher or using an incorrect coloured voucher.

Most dietary requirements are catered for at all catering stations, so please notify the server of your requirements.

Tap water

Tap water in Finland is of excellent quality and has been found to be significantly cleaner than bottled water. Drinking tap water is also more sustainable than buying bottled water. You can refill your bottle with cold water from any tap at the campus. The taps are mostly located in the lavatories.
